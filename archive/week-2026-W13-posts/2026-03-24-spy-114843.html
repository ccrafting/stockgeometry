SPY: Still Holding, But Tightening

Session date: 2026-03-24 | Session time (ET): 09:30 to 14:46

Returning to SPY later in the session, the broad tape looks less easy than it did in the first read. SPY is still organized around one main shape, but the support shelf has narrowed to Drift, Order-Flow Pressure, and Money Flow, while Volatility Pulse, Swing Move, Short Move, and Volume Shock have all moved onto the pressure side. That is a real shift from the earlier SPY read, where support was broader and volatility-related structure was still helping hold the session together.

Relative to the other reads, this later SPY sits between QQQ and DIA. It is not as tightly pressure-dominant as QQQ, because SPY still has a real support base underneath it. But it is also no longer as straightforwardly constructive as the first SPY read, and it is less cleanly price-led than DIA. The broad index now looks like a session that is still intact, but doing more work to stay that way.

The simplest shelf label is: SPY is still holding shape, but the session has tightened. Early support has thinned into a narrower floor, while strain has spread into the move itself. That leaves SPY broader than QQQ, but less comfortable than it looked earlier in the day.
