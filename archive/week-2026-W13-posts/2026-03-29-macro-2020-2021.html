2020-2021: Policy Cushion, Growth Tension

Session date: 2026-03-29 | Session time (ET): 09:30 to 23:59

Looking back across 2020-2021 as one macro object, the period did not read as a clean, evenly supported recovery. The clearest support came from Unemployment Rate and Fed Funds Rate, while the strongest strain came from QQQ High-Frequency Response. The rest of the object leaned burden-side as well: DIA Low-Frequency Anchor, 10-Year Treasury Yield, SPY Broad Surface Response, and Core PCE Inflation all sat on the pressure side.

That matters because the era was not internally calm just because it often looked powerful on the surface. The regime appears to have been held together by policy and labor support, while the fast-growth layer carried the deepest internal tension and the broader market surface never fully escaped that strain. In plain English, 2020-2021 looks less like a serene expansion than like a market being stabilized from underneath while speed, sensitivity, and speculative intensity kept the structure under pressure.
