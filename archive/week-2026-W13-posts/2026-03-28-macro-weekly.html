Macro: Inflation Pressure, Tech Strain

Session date: 2026-03-28 | Session time (ET): 09:30 to 16:00

Across the week ending March 27, the macro object did not read as a clean rates panic or a broad economic break. The clearest strain came from Core PCE Inflation and QQQ High-Frequency Response, while the Fed Funds Rate, Unemployment Rate, SPY Broad Surface Response, and DIA Low-Frequency Anchor sat on the stabilizing side. The 10-Year Treasury Yield was notably quieter in this read rather than acting as the main burden.

That matters because the week’s tension looks less like “everything macro is breaking at once” and more like a narrower conflict between persistent inflation pressure and fragility in the fast-growth tape. The slower policy-and-labor backdrop did not read as the thing tearing the structure apart. In plain English, the market still looks able to lean on its deeper anchors, but inflation pressure and the tech-sensitive layer are where the stress is showing up most clearly.
