SPY: Buyers Are Absorbing a Hot Tape

Session date: 2026-03-30 | Session time (ET): 09:30 to 11:50

A little over two hours into Monday's session, SPY has a thin, concentrated support base. Order-Flow Pressure and Money Flow are the only real stabilizers, and both are modest. Trend is essentially absent. On the other side, the strain stack is broad and much heavier: Swing Move is the main source of pressure, with Volume Shock and Volatility Pulse right behind it, plus a smaller contribution from the immediate move.

That mix usually reads as absorption underneath a tape that is still running hot. Buyers are present, and they are doing enough to keep the session upright, but the market's character is being set by move, activity, and instability rather than by a clean trend engine. For readers, the important point is that SPY still has sponsorship underneath it, yet the day is asking that sponsorship to do defensive work instead of letting it expand calmly.

Help

What this is

StockGeometry reads the internal construction of the move: what is acting as the floor, what is acting as the strain, and whether those pieces are organizing into a coherent posture or fighting each other. It tracks regime integrity — the geometry of support, pressure, participation, and instability — rather than surface headlines.

What this is not

This is not an order-book heatmap, a Level 2 or Level 3 execution tool, or a buy/sell signal bot. It is a structural read of how the session is being held together beneath the surface: what is making the move cohere, what is trying to tear it apart, and whether volatility is constructive, absorbed, or destructive.
