DOWROT: Strength Is Concentrated in Throughput and Execution

Session date: 2026-03-30 | Session time (ET): 09:30 to 14:15

This custom Dow check was built to answer a very specific question: where was Monday morning's Dow strength actually living? By the read, it was not evenly distributed across the whole credit-and-cyclicals complex. The strongest support came from Visa, Caterpillar, Home Depot, Travelers, and Goldman Sachs. JPMorgan was the clearest source of strain, with American Express only slightly on that side. That matters because it breaks the simple story. The morning bid was not a uniform "financials are strong" impulse. Support was concentrated in payment rails, industrial execution, housing-adjacent demand, insurance stability, and part of market-facing finance, while the money-center bank and consumer-credit pocket lagged behind.

That gives the earlier DIA signal more shape. DIA looked fully aligned at the index level, but once the index was opened up, the internal structure turned out to be selective. Strength was real, but it was not flat. Visa, Caterpillar, and Home Depot formed the clearest backbone, which is consistent with transaction throughput, real-economy execution, and spending capacity showing up together. Goldman Sachs and Travelers reinforced that structure from the finance side. JPMorgan failing to join them is the important exception, which leaves a less uniform bank-credit picture.

The deeper point is that this object was not static. The basket was mixed internally through the session, with a mixedness score of 0.692, a dominant-state fraction of 0.535, and 7 state transitions. So the support that finally showed up in the role stack was not one blunt block of buying. It moved between pockets before resolving on the supportive side. That makes this less like a single unified surge and more like a rotation into the parts of the Dow tied to throughput and execution, while the more direct bank-credit channel remained less convincing.

Help

What this is

StockGeometry reads the internal construction of the move: what is acting as the floor, what is acting as the strain, and whether those pieces are organizing into a coherent posture or fighting each other. It tracks regime integrity — the geometry of support, pressure, participation, and instability — rather than surface headlines.

What this is not

This is not an order-book heatmap, a Level 2 or Level 3 execution tool, or a buy/sell signal bot. It is a structural read of how the session is being held together beneath the surface: what is making the move cohere, what is trying to tear it apart, and whether volatility is constructive, absorbed, or destructive.
